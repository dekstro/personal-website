  • Developed a machine learning model to detect fake news articles by analyzing textual content and features.

  • Engineered relevant features and applied algorithms like Naive Bayes and Support Vector Machines (SVM) for classification.
  • Achieved an accuracy of 90% on the test dataset, demonstrating the model's effectiveness in distinguishing fake news.

The process of identifying and confirming the accuracy of news or information presented in media sources using a variety of techniques and technologies is known as fake news detection. Conducting a fake news detection project can help to promote accurate and trustworthy information, fight the spread of false narratives and propaganda, and protect the integrity of news and media sources. Detecting fake news is crucial in today's digital age where misinformation can spread quickly and have real-world consequences. The design of a fake news detection project using the decision tree algorithm entails analyzing and categorizing news articles based on their linguistic features and patterns. The algorithm then constructs a decision tree model that can classify news articles as either fake or real based on their attributes. Some of the findings on fake news detection are that the detection of fake news requires examining multiple factors, such as language, content, and sources, rather than relying solely on a single feature or attribute, according to some research findings. Decision trees, random forests, and neural networks are the machine learning algorithms that have been found to be successful in spotting fake news. Unfortunately, there are some research limitations: the decision tree algorithm relies on a predefined set of features, which may not be exhaustive enough to capture all the nuances and complexities of fake news detection. Incorporating additional features may require more extensive data pre-processing and feature engineering efforts. Decision trees can handle both categorical and continuous data and can be easily modified to incorporate new features and improve classification accuracy, making them a flexible and powerful tool for fake news detection. The originality and value of doing fake news detection using decision trees lie in its ability to provide an interpretable and transparent model for classifying news articles as either fake or real, which can help increase trust and confidence in the detection process.
